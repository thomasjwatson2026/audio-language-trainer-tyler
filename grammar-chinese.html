1) Core Sentence Order

Subject + Time + Place + Verb + Object

Example: Wǒ jīntiān zài jiā chīfàn.
I eat at home today.

2) No Verb Conjugation

Verbs usually do not change by person.

Wǒ qù. / Tā qù.
I go / He goes (same verb form).

3) Tense by Time Words

Use time words (yesterday/tomorrow/already) to show tense.

Wǒ zuótiān qù le. (I went yesterday.)
Wǒ míngtiān qù. (I will go tomorrow.)

4) Yes/No Questions with ma

Nǐ qù ma? (Are you going?)
Nǐ hǎo ma? (How are you?)

5) A-not-A Question Pattern

Alternative to ma.

Nǐ qù bú qù? (Going or not?)

6) Measure Words

Number + measure word + noun.

yí ge rén (one person), liǎng běn shū (two books)

7) Negation: bù vs méi

bù for habitual/present/future, méi for past / “did not”.

Wǒ bù chī ròu. (I don’t eat meat.)
Wǒ méi chī. (I didn’t eat.)

8) Possession with yǒu

Wǒ yǒu shuǐ. (I have water.)
Wǒ méiyǒu qián. (I don’t have money.)

9) Topic-comment structure

Put topic first for emphasis.

Zhège, wǒ xǐhuān. (This, I like.)

10) Location with zài

Tā zài jiā. (He is at home.)
Wǒ zài Měiguó xuéxí. (I study in the U.S.)

11) Completion particle le

Wǒ chī le. (I ate / I’m done eating.)

12) Progressive action with zài

Wǒ zài chīfàn. (I am eating.)

13) Modals for ability/want

Wǒ huì shuō Zhōngwén. (I can speak Chinese.)
Wǒ xiǎng qù. (I want to go.)

14) Common connectors

yīnwèi... suǒyǐ... (because... therefore...)
suīrán... dànshì... (although... but...)

15) Comparing with bǐ

Tā bǐ wǒ gāo.
He is taller than me.

16) Duration with le + time

Wǒ zài zhèlǐ zhù le liǎng nián.
I have lived here for two years.

17) Result complements

Wǒ tīng dǒng le. (I understood.)
Tā zuò wán le. (He finished doing it.)

18) Experience with guo

Wǒ qù guò Zhōngguó.
I have been to China.

19) Giving options with háishi

Nǐ hē chá háishi kāfēi?
Do you drink tea or coffee?

20) Polite requests with qǐng

Qǐng zài shuō yí biàn.
Please say it again.
